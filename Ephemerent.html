01 — Step one

First, make the work you can't watch legible — and trustworthy.

The model can write the function. That stopped being the hard part. The hard part is everything that happens while you're not looking: an agent edits across your repo, runs your tests, fixes what it broke — and you're asked to trust the result without ever seeing the work.

So step one is an environment where working with coding agents is legible and verifiable, not a black box you kick off and pray over. Every decision, test result and review stays in view; several attempts are tried and only the best, checked one gets merged back. That environment is Orrery — the first thing out of the lab, and the one that has to earn trust before anything further out gets to matter.

02 — The bet

Pull back, and the real bet is a different altitude.

The endgame of AI isn't a bigger language model. LLMs are one avenue — a powerful one — but mistaking them for the whole map is how you miss the territory. What I care about sits further out: systems that understand how things work, not just what word comes next.

Past the language model

LLMs are one road, not the map. Treat them as a tool rather than a religion, and the search stays open to whatever actually works.

World models, not word models

Multimodal systems that build a real model of how the world works — understanding and prediction grounded in things, not just the next token.

Compute in light

Eventually, substrates beyond today's GPUs — photonic computing, where the math happens in light rather than shuttled electrons.

Most of the industry is optimizing one curve. I'd rather go find the next one.
03 — Independent

Independent on purpose — so results, not fashion, get to decide.

It's a wild thing to say as a one-person, no-funding operation. I know. But that's exactly why I'm staying independent: I don't have to pick one religion and defend it to a board. I get to explore, follow the evidence across approaches, and let results decide instead of fashion.

It's early, and ambitious to the point of absurd. That's the point — the next curve doesn't get found by people who can only afford to defend the current one.
